The Crest

The crest is a sheild divided into 4 quartes, each representing the houses. The latin mottom at the bottom says "Draco dormiens nunquam titillandus" meaning "Never tickle a sleeping dragon."

Hogwarts Castle

The castle was built over a thousand years before Harry Potter's time by a wizard architect and the four most celebrated wizards of the age: Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw and Salazar Slytherin.[7] The castle was the main building of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, regarded as the finest wizarding school in the world.

The series

The seven Harry Potter books in order are Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997), Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (1998), Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (1999), Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (2000), Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (2003), Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2005), and Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (2007).

Fun Facts

  • J.K. Rowling and Harry Potter share a birthday: July 31st.
  • Rowling came up with the Hogwarts house names on the back of a barf bag.
  • Dumbledore means “bumblebee” in Old English.
  • Arthur Weasley was originally planned to die in the series.
  • The series has sold 500+ million copies and been translated into 80 languages.
  • The Hogwarts Express was inspired by a real Scottish steam train.
